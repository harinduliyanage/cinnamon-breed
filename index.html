Who We Are

Breed cinnamon tradings is operating in value adding for our main trading commodity Cinnamon where carrying out its activities of collecting pure Ceylon cinnamon and process in the form of pure cinnamon powder, cut and bale cinnamon in different sizes and packaging them accordance with the requirement to be filled with forging market requirements and fulfill the demand of main larger importers in the world. The business is carrying out it’s main activities in the area of Sothern province in Sri Lanka with the opportunity of the area grow the larger fraction of Sri Lanka’s Cinnamon cultivation that is concentrated in beautiful coastal town Ahungalla and Kosgoda in the Sothern costal belt in Sri Lanka.

What's the Ceylon Cinnamon or true cinnamon.

It's made from the inner bark of the Cinnamomum verum tree. Ceylon cinnamon is the expensive, rare, safe and recommended variety over all other varieties including Cassia, Korintje and Saigon. True cinnamon is graded as the fourth most expensive spice in the world. 80%-90% of world’s production of this product belongs to a small Asian country, known as Sri Lanka.

Health Benefits of Cinnamon!

  • 1/2 teaspoon of cinnamon per day can lower LDL cholesterol.
  • True Cinnamon has controlling effect on blood sugar, making it especially helpful for people with Type 2 diabetes.
  • True Cinnamon has an amazing capability to stop medication-resistant yeast infections.
  • In a study published by researchers at the U.S. Department of Agriculture in Maryland, true cinnamon reduced the proliferation of leukemia and lymphoma cancer cells. 
  • True Cinnamon’s antioxidant qualities have been applied in oxidative stress illnesses, where Cinnamon tea has been prescribed. And Cinnamon oil is used in pastes used against skin infections and surface wounds due to its antifungal, antibacterial, antiviral, anti-parasitic and antiseptic properties.
  • It has an anti-clotting effect on the blood.
  • In a study at Copenhagen University, patients given half a teaspoon of cinnamon powder combined with one tablespoon of honey every morning before breakfast had significant relief in arthritis pain after one week and could walk without pain within one month.
  • When added to food, it inhibits bacterial growth and food spoilage, making it a natural food preservative.
  • One study found that smelling cinnamon boosts cognitive function and memory. 
  • Researchers at Kansas State University found that cinnamon fights the E. coli bacteria in unpasteurized juices. 
  • It is a great source of manganese, fiber, iron, and calcium.

Cinnamon's unique healing abilities come from three basic types of components in the essential oils found in its bark. These oils contain active components called cinnamaldehyde, cinnamyl acetate, and cinnamyl alcohol, plus a wide range of other volatile substances
